Black Label Media Website

Over the summer I worked at a Production Company called Black Label Media. Its producer, Molly Smith, is famous for The Blind Side, and while I was there the company released Begin Again and The Good Lie.

In my downtime I worked on a website for them as a personal project. It was challenging working with a dark theme, but by using thinner fonts and allowing for more blank space I acheived a very clean, simplistic look that I am happy with. Limitations in the content that the company had on hand (images, videos, etc) prevented me from finishing the site, but it was a fun project nontheless.

  • Client: Black Label Media
  • Date: Summer 2014
  • Service: Web Development

Sigma Nu Bubble Soccer



"Bubble Soccer" was a philanthropy event put on by my fraternity to raise money for the Evanston Animal Shelter. The entire campaign I envisioned consisted of very minimalist photos (profile pictures and cover photos on social media) with YouTube links to different Bubble Soccer videos, and the website built above.
We coupled this with some live interest-generation by having brothers stand at different spots on campus wearing the inflated bubbles, completely covered in flyers. While non-bubbled brothers spoke to people passing by and encouraging them to attend.

The campaign was wildly successful, with just under 200 people braving the weather in December to come attend. Hundreds of dollars were raised.

  • Client: Sigma Nu
  • Date: Winter 2014
  • Service: Ad Campaign

Music

I have been a musician for my entire life. I've sung for presidents, with world-class orchestras, and on-stage with Beyoncé at the 77th Academy Awards. I write music for piano and guitar, and have a hobby of writing songs with comedy lyrics, or re-writing existing song's lyrics.


These are just two specific and more professional projects that I very much enjoyed creating.

As just a small part of a project for a class, I was tasked with finding music to be played with the reading of a scene from Tennessee Williams' The Glass Menagerie>. I was unable to find music that I felt fit the scene perfectly, so I arranged some of my own using GarageBand.


[MUSIC]

The play is memory.

Being a memory play, it is dimly lighted, it is sentimental, it is not realistic.

In memory everything seems to happen to music. That explains the fiddle in the wings.

I am the narrator of the play, and also a character in it. The other characters are my mother Amanda, my sister Laura and a gentleman caller who appears in the final scenes.

He is the most realistic character in the play, being an emissary from a world of reality that we were somehow set apart from. But since I have a poet's weakness for symbols, I am using this character also as a symbol; he is the long-delayed but always expected something that we live for. There is a fifth character in the play who doesn't appear except in this larger-than-life-size photograph over the mantel.

This is our father who left us a long time ago.He was a telephone man who fell in love with long distances; he gave up his job with the telephone company and skipped the light fantastic out of town. . . .The last we heard of him was a picture postcard from Mazatlan, on the Pacific coast of Mexico, containing a message of two words -
'Hello - Good-bye!' and no address.

I think the rest of the play will explain itself ...

Tag ALS

In spring of 2014, Sigma Nu paired up with Alpha Phi sorority on campus to raise money for the Les Turner ALS foundation by canning in Evanston. I worked with my committee to create this cover photo for the campaign. We successfully raised nearly $2000 just from canning, because huge numbers of people attended in multiple locations, and there was no cost.
It was a clear demonstration that a simple solution can be the most effective.

  • Client: Les Turner ALS Foundation
  • Date: May 2014
  • Service: Social Media Campaign

Kickin' It

Kickin' It is the largest philanthropy effort that my fraternity chapter makes each year. The event is a massive beach soccer tournament with DJs, beach games, beach volleyball and unlimited food. 100% of the proceeds went to Sigma Nu's national beneficiary: St. Jude's Children's Hospital. I worked with my committee to design the above cover photo, and persoanlly reached out to both the Chicago Blackhawks and Chipotle, who both agreed to help sponsor the event.

Marshmallow Madness

Dance Marathon is one of the largest student-run philanthropies in the nation. Each year on campus, Greek organizations team up in fraternity-sorority pairs to raise money for the cause.

This flyer was designed to promote an event that Sigma Nu had with our partner Kappa Delta to raise money for DM. The clean blue flyer and profile picture caught people's attention and managed to make many students change their course across campus to stop by for a cup of hot chocolate.

  • Client: Sigma Nu/Northwestern Dance Marathon
